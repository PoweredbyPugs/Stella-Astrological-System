## I. Containment

There is a house that was built before
anyone moved in. The door
was hung before the knock. The table set
before the guest was named.
Someone poured the foundation in the dark
and called it faith — then waited
for the faith to justify the pour.
The walls are load-bearing.
The walls are all there is.

## II. Periodicity

The laundry cycle has no narrative.
Soiled, then clean, then soiled.
The man who floods rebuilds.
The man who rebuilds floods.
Between the two a frequency is born —
not chosen, not refused,
the way a lung does not decide to breathe
but breathes, and calls it living,
and does not ask permission from the air.

## III. Threshold

The thought arrives from somewhere
it does not own a passport.
At the border there are ten logs
stacked beneath an arch.
On this side: countable. Named.
On the other: woods.
The thinker stands between them
feeding birds that would have starved
without his particular confusion,
his willingness to carry seed
to a place he cannot verify exists.
