# Zodiacal Releasing Reference

Primary sources: Vettius Valens (Anthology, Books IV-VII), Chris Brennan (Hellenistic Astrology)

## Core Technique

ZR divides life into chapters using the Lot of Spirit (career/purpose) and Lot of Fortune (health/circumstances). Each sign is assigned years based on its planetary ruler's period:

| Sign | Years | Ruler |
|------|-------|-------|
| Cancer | 25 | Moon |
| Leo | 19 | Sun |
| Gemini, Virgo | 20 | Mercury |
| Taurus, Libra | 8 | Venus |
| Aries, Scorpio | 15 | Mars |
| Pisces, Sagittarius | 12 | Jupiter |
| Capricorn | 27 | Saturn |
| Aquarius | 30 | Saturn |

## Peak Periods (from Lot of Fortune)

The four **angular signs from Fortune** mark peak periods:

1. **10th from Fortune** = Major Peak (💕💕)
2. **Fortune itself (1st)** = Major Peak (💕)
3. **7th from Fortune** = Moderate Peak (❤️)
4. **4th from Fortune** = Minor Peak (💕)

These form the basis of *when* activity intensifies. The modality of Fortune determines which signs are peaks:
- Fortune in Mutable → Mutable signs are peaks
- Fortune in Fixed → Fixed signs are peaks
- Fortune in Cardinal → Cardinal signs are peaks

## Angular Triads

Each sequence of three signs forms a triad:
1. **Preparatory** (beginning) — anticipatory, building toward
2. **Peak** (middle, Fortune angle) — heightened activity
3. **Completion** (end) — carrying forward, bringing to close

This creates 4 triads cycling through the zodiac. Events initiated in preparatory signs become active at peaks and resolve in completion signs.

## Quality of Periods (Sect)

The *quality* of a period depends on which planets aspect or occupy the activated sign:

### Day Charts:
- **Jupiter** (in-sect benefic) = Most positive periods
- **Venus** (contrary-sect benefic) = Moderately positive
- **Saturn** (in-sect malefic) = Surmountable difficulties
- **Mars** (contrary-sect malefic) = Most challenging

### Night Charts:
- **Venus** (in-sect benefic) = Most positive periods
- **Jupiter** (contrary-sect benefic) = Moderately positive
- **Mars** (in-sect malefic) = Surmountable difficulties
- **Saturn** (contrary-sect malefic) = Most challenging

## Interpreting Periods (Valens' Priority)

From Valens, Anthology 4:

1. **Ruler of the activated sign** — its condition, dignity, aspects
2. **Planets placed IN the activated sign** — most direct influence
3. **Planets configured (aspecting) the activated sign** — testimony
4. **Condition of the ruler** — dignity, sect, house placement

**Aspect influence ranking** (Brennan's experience + Hellenistic doctrine):
1. Planet IN the sign (strongest)
2. Superior square (overcomes)
3. Opposition
4. Inferior square (weakest)

## Loosing of the Bond

When L2 completes a full cycle and returns to L1's sign, it "jumps" to the sign **opposite** L1 instead of continuing. This indicates:

- **Career transitions**
- **Major life direction changes**
- **Pivotal moments**

### Context matters:
- Loosing in **preparatory sign** → transition to new path
- Loosing in **peak sign** → major achievement/recognition
- Loosing in **completion sign** → wrapping up, integrating

## Subperiods

Each level subdivides the period above it:
- **L1** = General periods (years/decades)
- **L2** = Subperiods (months/years)
- **L3** = Sub-subperiods (weeks/months)
- **L4** = Days
- **L5** = Hours

The same ZR logic applies at each level — peaks, triads, ruler conditions.

## What ZR Does NOT Include

ZR is a **timing technique** only. It identifies *when* and the *quality* of periods.

Do NOT layer on:
- Transit interpretations (separate technique)
- Progressions (separate technique)
- Solar returns (separate technique)
- Modern psychological overlays

When using ZR, interpret through:
- The natal chart condition of activated signs/rulers
- The sect-based benefic/malefic hierarchy
- The angular triad position (prep/peak/completion)
- Loosing of the bond if applicable
